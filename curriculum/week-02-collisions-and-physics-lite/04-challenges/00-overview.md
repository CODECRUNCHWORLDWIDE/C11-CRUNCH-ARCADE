# Week 2 — Challenges

The exercises drill basics. **Challenges stretch you.** Each takes 90–120 minutes and produces something you can commit to your portfolio repo.

## Index

1. **[Challenge 1 — Brick Breaker Prototype](./challenge-01-brick-breaker.md)** — a paddle, a ball, ten bricks, the canonical "break the bricks" loop. The bones of Atari Breakout in ~200 lines. Solves the same collision problem your mini-project does, but in a constrained sandbox so you can get the physics right before adding score, lives, and polish. (~120 min)

Challenges are optional. If you skip them you can still pass the week. If you do them, you'll be measurably ahead of someone who didn't — and the mini-project will feel obvious instead of intimidating.

## How to work the challenges

- Read the spec end-to-end before you write a single line. Skim, don't memorise.
- Write the smallest version first. A ball that bounces off the paddle beats a brick wall that doesn't bounce.
- Run it after every change. If you write 100 lines without running, three of those bugs are mating in the dark.
- Commit at every working milestone. "It compiles" is not a milestone; "it bounces off the paddle" is.
- After it works, *then* attempt the stretches. Get the spec green first.

Challenges live under your portfolio repo at `c11-week-02-<yourhandle>/challenges/challenge-01/`. The mini-project repo is separate.
