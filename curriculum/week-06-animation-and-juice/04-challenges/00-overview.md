# Week 6 — Challenges

The exercises drill the systems. **Challenges stretch you toward shippable artefacts.** This week's challenge is the deliverable everyone in industry recognises: a *side-by-side before-and-after video* of your Week-5 character with and without juice, accompanied by a 200-word teardown of which juice tricks earned their keep and which were noise.

This is the format Nijman uses in *The Art of Screenshake*. It is the format Jonasson and Purho use in *Juice it or lose it*. It is the format Vlambeer, *Hyper Light Drifter*, *Celeste*, and every game-feel-aware studio uses when arguing for polish budget with management. Internalising the format is how you stop saying "it feels better" and start saying "the screen-shake on landings added 60% of the impact; the chromatic-aberration on hit was noise; here's why."

## Index

1. **[Challenge 1 — Before/after juice comparison](./challenge-01-before-after-juice-comparison.md)** — record a side-by-side comparison video of your Week-5 character (no juice) and your Exercise-3 character (with juice). Run the same input sequence on both. Write a 200-word teardown that ranks the juice tricks by perceived impact. The artefact is the *video plus the writeup*; the engineering work is mostly tuning. (~90-120 min including recording, editing, and writing.)

Challenges are optional. If you skip this one you can still pass the week — but the comparison video is the single most useful portfolio piece you'll produce in C11 before the Week-11 capstone. It is also the format you'll be asked to deliver in any interview involving game feel. Doing it once now means you have the template forever.

## How to work the challenges

- Read the spec end-to-end before you open your screen recorder.
- **Run the same input sequence on both versions.** Same path, same jumps, same spike hit. If the inputs are different the comparison is invalid.
- **Use OBS Studio** (free, cross-platform) for the recording. The setup takes three minutes; the recording takes thirty seconds per take.
- **Side-by-side composition** beats two clips stitched end-to-end. The eye compares spatially better than temporally. Any free video editor (DaVinci Resolve, Shotcut, OpenShot) does side-by-side composition in two clicks.
- Commit the video AND the writeup. The writeup is the engineering artefact; the video is the demo.

Challenges live under your portfolio repo at `c11-week-06-<yourhandle>/challenges/challenge-01/`. The mini-project repo is separate but will reuse the same comparison-video pattern as its Sunday deliverable.
