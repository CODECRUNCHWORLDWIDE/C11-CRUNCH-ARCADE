# Week 1 — Challenges

The exercises drill basics. **Challenges stretch you.** Each takes 60–120 minutes and produces something you can commit to your portfolio repo.

## Index

1. **[Challenge 1 — Bouncing Ball](./challenge-01-bouncing-ball.md)** — a circle that bounces off all four window edges, with delta-time-correct velocity reflection and a couple of polish touches that separate a prototype from a *feeling-right* prototype. (~90 min)

Challenges are optional. If you skip them, you can still pass the week. If you do them, you'll be measurably ahead of someone who didn't — and the mini-project will feel obvious instead of intimidating.

## How to work the challenges

- Read the spec end-to-end before you write a single line. Skim, don't memorise.
- Write the smallest version first. A bouncing ball at constant velocity beats a non-bouncing ball with five tuned parameters.
- Run it after every change. If you write 100 lines without running, three of those bugs are mating in the dark.
- Commit at every working milestone. "It compiles" is not a milestone; "it bounces" is.
- After it works, *then* attempt the stretches. Get the spec green first.

Challenges live under your portfolio repo at `c11-week-01-<yourhandle>/challenges/challenge-01/`. The mini-project repo is separate.
